Three laws of robotics

Rules developed by science-fiction writer Isaac Asimov, who
sought to create an ethical system for humans and robots.

1st Law

A robot may not injure a human being or,
through inaction, allow a human being to come
to harm

2nd Law

A robot must obey the orders given it by human
beings except where such orders would conflict
with the First Law

3rd Law

A robot must protect its own existence as long
as such protection does not conflict with the
First or Second Law

Asimov later added another rule, known as the fourth or zeroth law, that superseded the others. It stated that “a
robot may not harm humanity, or, by inaction, allow humanity to come to harm.”
